My professional identity

I enjoy finding solutions to problems and implementing ideas through clever design. This is something any engineer might say. What a typical engineer might neglect though, is to think about ways to incorporate sleek looks into a design. This is where I place myself as a designer. I am interested in blending robust and effective engineering solutions with eye-pleasing aesthetics

I enjoy team leading, finding satisfaction in providing direction, guidance and support and working together towards an outcome that would not have been possible without the combination of each person’s strengths and insights. As a student, I am most competent in the area of expertise ‘Technology and Realization’. That said, I still aim to further develop and specialize myself in this area. Furthermore, I want to develop myself in the areas ‘Creativity and Aesthetics’ as well as ‘Math, Data and Computing’. The remaining two areas still hold their importance, yet are not my priority.

My vision

I see design becoming increasingly important with each year passing by. In the past, there
wasn’t a whole lot of choice in what people could buy and so they would settle for what was
available. Today, people have a vast range of products to choose from and so, products lacking
industrial design do not have much of a chance to succeed.

Even so, there is still much room for improvement in various industries. Today’s products are
often still difficult and unintuitive to use and even a bright mind can sometimes struggle to
operate them. I wish to improve this. What is simple should be simple to use. That said, I want
to avoid the simplification of products at the expense of the functionality offered, which seems
to be the trend with a number of products and services today. Making a complex thing simple
and enjoyable to use without sacrificing any of its features should be an important design
goal.

My future path is not yet set. There are far too many industries I would be potentially
interested to work in and to name some would mean to disqualify others. Therefore, I wish to
leave all my doors open for now, while I continue looking for ‘the one’
